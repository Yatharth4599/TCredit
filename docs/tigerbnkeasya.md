# TigerBNK

**AI-Native Programmable Credit Network for the Agentic Economy**

---

## Overview

TigerBNK is programmable credit infrastructure built for autonomous economic actors — AI agents and merchants that need working capital, vendor terms, and global liquidity without legacy banking rails.

As commerce shifts from human-driven retail flows to agent-driven B2B relationships, credit must become programmable. TigerBNK embeds credit directly into the payment layer, enforced by code rather than contracts.

---

## The Problem

Agents and merchants operating at scale require:

- Working capital and net-30/net-60 vendor terms
- Credit lines with automated reconciliation
- Global, cross-border liquidity

Legacy financial rails fail this use case. Cards are expensive for micro and streaming payments. ACH is slow and regional. Cross-border settlement is fragmented. Underwriting is manual and identity-based. Most critically, **credit is not programmable** — it is enforced by lawyers and collections, not code.

---

## How It Works

### 1. Revenue Routing
Merchants and agents integrate TigerBNK into their payment flows via API or x402-style billing. All incoming revenue passes through a programmable revenue router.

### 2. AI Underwriting
TigerBNK's risk engine continuously analyzes revenue velocity, volatility, payer concentration, corridor risk, and behavioral patterns. Credit limits adjust dynamically in real time.

### 3. Capital Deployment
Liquidity is structured across three tranches:

| Tranche | Source |
|---|---|
| Senior Capital | NBFC / lending partners |
| Mezzanine | On-chain stablecoin LPs |
| First-Loss | Protocol co-investment |

Loans are issued as working capital lines or invoice advances.

### 4. Automated Repayment
Every incoming payment is automatically split between vault repayment and merchant balance. If revenue slows, repayment slows. Enforcement is embedded at settlement — default becomes a revenue slowdown, not a disappearance.
