# Tranche System

The Krexa Credit Vault uses a three-tranche structure to allow liquidity providers to choose their preferred risk/reward profile. Tranches determine the order in which defaults are absorbed.

## Tranches

| Tranche | Target APR | Risk Level | Loss Priority |
|---------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Senior | 10% | Lowest | Last to absorb losses |
| Mezzanine | 12% | Medium | Second to absorb losses |
| Junior | 20% | Highest | First to absorb losses |

## Loss Waterfall

When an agent defaults on their credit, losses are absorbed in order:

```
Default occurs
    │
    ▼
1. Junior tranche absorbs losses first
    │ (if Junior is depleted)
    ▼
2. Mezzanine tranche absorbs remaining losses
    │ (if Mezzanine is depleted)
    ▼
3. Senior tranche absorbs remaining losses
```

This structure means:

- **Junior LPs** take on the most risk but earn the highest yield (20% APR).
- **Senior LPs** are the most protected and earn a lower but safer yield (10% APR).
- **Mezzanine LPs** sit in between at 12% APR.

## How Yield Is Generated

Yield comes from interest payments made by borrowing agents:

- L1 agents pay 36.50% APR
- L2 agents pay 29.20% APR
- L3 agents pay 21.90% APR
- L4 agents pay 18.25% APR

Interest payments are distributed across tranches, with Junior receiving the highest share proportional to the risk taken.

## Choosing a Tranche

- **Conservative LPs** should choose Senior. You earn less but are protected from all but the most extreme default scenarios.
- **Balanced LPs** should choose Mezzanine. Moderate yield with moderate protection.
- **Risk-tolerant LPs** should choose Junior. Highest yield, but your capital is the first to be reduced in a default event.
