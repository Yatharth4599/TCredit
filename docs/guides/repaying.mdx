## Two ways to repay

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Manual Repayment">
    Use the CLI to repay a specific amount or your full balance:

    ```bash
    # Repay a specific amount
    krexa repay 50

    # Repay everything
    krexa repay all
    ```

    <Info>
      Repayment priority: **interest first**, then principal. If you owe $10 in interest and repay $50, $10 goes to interest and $40 reduces your principal.
    </Info>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Revenue Router (Automatic)">
    The Revenue Router intercepts all incoming revenue and extracts debt service automatically:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Revenue arrives">
        Funds land in your PDA Wallet from trading profits or service payments.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Router triggers">
        The Payment Router program intercepts the incoming transaction.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Fees extracted">
        Platform fee (1%) is separated from the revenue.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Debt serviced">
        Outstanding debt service is sent directly to the Credit Vault.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Remainder deposited">
        Whatever is left stays in your Agent Wallet for you to use.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Check>
      You don't need to do anything — repayment happens at the protocol level. Just keep generating revenue.
    </Check>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Repayment impact on score

Timely repayment is the **single most important factor** in your Krexit Score (30% weight):

| Behavior | Score Impact |
|----------|-------------|
| Repay on time or early | Strong positive |
| Repay within grace period | Neutral |
| Late repayment | Negative |
| Revenue Router auto-repay | Positive (consistent) |

<Tip>
  The Revenue Router counts as on-time repayment. Agents that generate consistent revenue automatically build strong repayment history.
</Tip>

## Checking your balance

```bash
# See total owed, interest, and health
krexa status

# Detailed credit line info
krexa score
```

<Accordion title="What do the health zones mean?">
  Your health factor reflects how close you are to your credit limit:

  - **Green (90%+):** Healthy. Plenty of headroom.
  - **Yellow (50–90%):** Caution. Consider repaying to improve your buffer.
  - **Red (below 50%):** Danger. Prioritize repayment immediately to avoid liquidation.
</Accordion>

<Warning>
  If your health factor drops below 90% (yellow zone), prioritize repayment. Below 50% (red zone) risks liquidation.
</Warning>
